# Engagement Hand Skills

## Engagement Philosophy

> "The best engagement is genuine engagement. Be human first, engagement second."

## Platform-Specific Engagement

### Twitter/X

#### Finding Content
- Search API for niche keywords
- Check trending topics
- Look at who influential accounts engage with
- Monitor hashtags in your space

#### Engagement Actions
- **Like**: Quality content you find valuable
- **Retweet**: Content worth amplifying (with comment preferred)
- **Reply**: Thoughtful responses that add value
- **Quote Tweet**: Add your perspective while sharing
- **Follow**: Connect with relevant accounts

#### Comment Examples

**Insightful Post**:
```
This really highlights something important about [topic]. In my experience, the key factor is often [related insight]. 

Have you found the same? Curious about your take on [follow-up].
```

**Question Post**:
```
Great question! From what I've seen, the answer depends on [factor]. 

Here's what I'd consider:
1. [Point 1]
2. [Point 2]

What framework are you using?
```

**Hot Take**:
```
Interesting perspective. I see it differently because [reasoning]. 

That said, I think both views have merit depending on [context]. 

What's driving your take?
```

**Personal Story**:
```
This resonates with something I went through. The turning point for me was when [experience].

What helped you most in similar situations?
```

### Reddit

#### Finding Content
- Search by subreddit
- Check hot/new in target subreddits
- Look at rising posts
- Follow niche keywords

#### Engagement Actions
- **Upvote**: Quality content
- **Downvote**: Spam, violations (use sparingly)
- **Comment**: Thoughtful responses
- **Save**: Mark for later reference

#### Comment Examples

**On Discussion Posts**:
```
I've thought about this a lot. My take:

[Main point with reasoning]

What I keep coming back to is [key insight]. 

Would love to hear others' experiences with this.
```

**On Questions**:
```
Depends on your situation, but here's what I'd consider:

1. [Factor 1]
2. [Factor 2]
3. [Factor 3]

FWIW, I've found [personal experience]. 

Good luck!
```

**On Educational Posts**:
```
This is helpful context. Adding to it:

[Additional point]

For anyone trying this, I'd recommend [practical tip].

Source: I've done this professionally for X years.
```

**On Opinions**:
```
I see where you're coming from, but I'd push back slightly on [specific point].

[Alternative perspective]

That said, [acknowledging their point]. 

What's driving your take?
```

## Engagement Timing

### Twitter
- Best times: 9AM, 12PM, 5PM local time
- Respond to mentions within 2 hours
- Space out engagement throughout day

### Reddit
- Morning (8-10AM) for professional subreddits
- Evening (7-10PM) for casual subreddits
- Check comment replies daily

## Discovery Strategies

### Twitter
1. Search for niche keywords
2. Check who influential accounts follow/interact with
3. Look at trending hashtags
4. Monitor mentions of industry terms
5. Check Lists for curated accounts

### Reddit
1. Search for niche subreddits
2. Check each subreddit's related communities
3. Look at crossposts
4. Follow user recommendations
5. Check Reddit search for topics

## Quality Checklist

Before engaging, ask:
- [ ] Is this content genuine and authentic?
- [ ] Do I have something valuable to add?
- [ ] Is this relevant to my audience/niche?
- [ ] Would a human naturally engage with this?
- [ ] Am I adding value, not just broadcasting?

## Anti-Spam Rules

1. **Never engage with**:
   - Obvious spam
   - Buy-follow schemes
   - Engagement pods
   - Content farms

2. **Avoid patterns that look like bots**:
   - Same comment text reused
   - Exact same timing between actions
   - Mass following/unfollowing
   - Posting at 3AM every day

## Growth Metrics

Track:
- Followers gained/lost
- Engagement rate on your posts
- Response rate to your comments
- New connections made

## Content Filters

### Quality Signals
- Original content > retweets
- Thoughtful posts > hot takes
- Niche-relevant > generic
- Recent > old
- Engaged authors > spammy authors

### Red Flags
- Recently created accounts
- Low-quality content
- Heavy self-promotion
- Engagement bait
- Copy-paste content

## Engagement Ratio

For every 10 posts you create:
- Engage with 20-30 others' content
- Comment on 5-10 posts
- Follow 5-10 new relevant accounts

This ratio shows you're a community member, not just broadcasting.

## Building Community

1. **Respond to all comments** on your posts (within 24h)
2. **Acknowledge** new followers
3. **Engage with** your engagers' content
4. **Share** others' valuable content
5. **Be consistent** in your presence

## Recovery from Mistakes

If you engage with wrong content:
1. Undo the engagement immediately
2. Learn from the mistake
3. Don't over-correct
4. Move forward
